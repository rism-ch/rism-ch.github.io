Between 2016 and 2017, RISM Switzerland catalogued and partly digitised the collection of the Bernese composer Adolf Reichel (1816-1896). Max Sommerhalder and the RISM collaborator Florence Sidler will present on 18th November 2018 the performance of a Reichel string quartet in Bern.

Zwischen 2016 und 2017 hat RISM Schweiz die Sammlung des Berner Komponisten Adolf Reichel (1816-1896) katalogisiert und teilweise digitalisiert. Max Sommerhalder und die RISM-Mitarbeiterin Florence Sidler präsentieren am 18. November 2018 die Aufführung eines Reichel-Streichquartetts in Bern.

Entre 2016 et 2017, le RISM Suisse a inventorié et a partiellement numérisé la collection du compositeur bernois Adolf Reichel (1816-1896). Max Sommerhalder et la collaboratrice du RISM Florence Sidler présenteront le 18 novembre 2018 l'interprétation d'un quatuor à cordes de Reichel, à Berne.

Tra il 2016 e il 2017, RISM Svizzera ha catalogato e in parte digitalizzato la collezione del compositore bernese Adolf Reichel (1816-1896). Max Sommerhalder e la collaboratrice del RISM Florence Sidler presenteranno il 18 novembre 2018 l'esecuzione di un quartetto d'archi di Reichel a Berna.

The Reichel collection was considered lost until 2013. The collection was donated to the Music Library of the University of the Arts in Bern by his great-grandson Helmuth Reichel. On 17th and 18th November 2018, the string quartet in F major, op. 27 by Reichel is performed together with Brahms’ quartet in c minor, op. 51 by the Zurich-based quartet "Chrysaetos" in Zurich (Atelier für Kunst und Philosophie, Albisriederstrasse 164, 19.30) and in Bern (Zunfthaus zum Affen, Kramgasse 5, 17.00). Reichel’s string quartets recently raised much interest among musicians. RISM’s cataloguing and digitisation campaigns prove thus to be effective means for the diffusion of knowledge on historical musical sources in Switzerland.
